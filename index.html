I am a postdoctoral fellow at the University of Copenhagen. I study electoral corruption, electoral systems, clientelism, and institutional manipulation. I analyze large, complex administrative data, including granular polling station results, censuses, voter registers, and official budgets. In collaboration with external partners, I have recently designed and implemented large-scale field experiments in Denmark to study satisfaction with the bureaucracy and how to correct misperceptions with statistics. I am a keen user of R stats and I have taught courses on statistics and programming in addition to substantive courses on comparative politics to undergraduate and graduates. I have also supervised masters theses.
